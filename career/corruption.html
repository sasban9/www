📌 The Mission

I am setting out on an indefinite journey across India — every state, every district, every village and town — to collect and highlight real stories of everyday corruption. Stories where ordinary people were forced to pay bribes: ₹50 for a signature, ₹500 for a birth certificate, ₹5,000 for a job application, or even ₹5 lakh for a contract.

“It’s not just the big scams that break this country — it’s the daily silence around small injustice.”

🚶 The Journey

  • 🌍 Covering all 750+ districts, 6000+ towns, and countless villages
  • 🛺 Traveling by public transport, hitchhiking, walking – no VIP convoys
  • 🎤 Talking to real people in local languages
  • 📷 Capturing every story on video, photo, and written word
  • 🧾 Verifying documents wherever possible (RTI, receipts, affidavits)

🗣️ What Will Be Highlighted?

Every time someone had to pay a bribe or "manage someone inside" to:

  • Get a ration card or birth certificate
  • Register land or property
  • Enroll a child in school
  • Join a government job or service
  • Receive a government scheme benefit

📢 What Happens Next?

Each story will be published on a public platform. Names can be hidden if needed. Where possible, we’ll help people file RTIs, complaints, or legal notices. This isn’t just a trip. It’s a people-powered movement.

🛑 Because Silence is Not an Option

We’ve normalized the “chai-pani” culture. But it’s time to speak up. Not with rage, but with truth. Every ₹50 bribe is a broken system. Every silent victim deserves a voice.

“This is not about politics. This is about people.”

🌟 Timeline of Anti-Corruption Heroes

2003 – Satyendra Dubey

Exposed corruption in highway projects; tragically assassinated while working for transparency.

2005 – Manjunath Shanmugam

Killed for sealing a corrupt petrol station while working with Indian Oil.

2005 – Aruna Roy & MKSS

RTI movement gains national attention with grassroots mobilization.

2011 – Anna Hazare & India Against Corruption

Massive civil movement demanding the Jan Lokpal Bill. Sparked national anti-corruption discourse.

2012 – Arvind Kejriwal forms Aam Aadmi Party

Brings anti-corruption agenda to electoral politics, emerging from IAC movement.

2015 – Sanjiv Chaturvedi wins Magsaysay Award

Recognized for exposing corruption in AIIMS and standing firm in public service reforms.

2019 & beyond – Digital Activism

Platforms like The Wire, Alt News, Scroll.in uncover corruption via investigative journalism.
