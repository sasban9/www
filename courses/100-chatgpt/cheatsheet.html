Prompt Structure Breakdown

  1. Goal: The main objective or purpose of the prompt.
  2. Result Format: Output formatting like code blocks, tables, lists.
  3. Warnings/Cautions: Constraints to ensure accuracy, tone, bias, ethics, or factual reliability.
  4. Context Dump: Background info to make the prompt clear and useful.

Prompting Techniques

  1. Be Specific and Clear: Provide detailed instructions. Instead of "Tell me about dogs," say "Give me a summary of the 5 most popular dog breeds in the US in 2023, including their sizes and temperaments."
  2. Use Role or Context: Frame the situation. E.g., "I'm the social media strategist for a startup. What are trending ideas for our campaign?"
  3. Ask for Step-by-Step Explanations: E.g., "Explain how to bake a sourdough loaf step by step for a beginner."
  4. Summarize: Ask it to condense long text: "Summarize this article in 3 points."
  5. Break Complex Questions into Parts: Use "First, Second, Third" structure.
  6. Use Examples or Analogies: E.g., "Act as a teacher and explain climate change using a simple analogy."
  7. Ask for Lists or Tables: E.g., "Give me a table of pros and cons for electric vs gas cars."
  8. Compare Multiple Options: E.g., "Compare the environmental impact of three types of reusable water bottles."

Prompting Frameworks

  • ERA (Expectation, Role, Action)
  • APEX (Action, Purpose, Expectation)
  • RAISE (Role, Action, Information, Style, Expectation)
  • CRAFT (Context, Role, Action, Format, Tone)
  • SOFT (Structure, Objective, Format, Tone)
  • CRISP (Context, Role, Intent, Specifics, Persona)
  • FIRE (Format, Instruction, Role, Example)
  • DARE (Describe, Action, Role, Example)
  • RISE (Role, Input, Steps, Example)

Key Terms

  • Deliberate Alignment: Step-by-step chain-of-thought prompting to reduce hallucinated output.
  • Top-p (Nucleus Sampling): Limits taken selection to a probability threshold.
  • Temperature: Controls output randomness. 0 = more consistent & repeatable, 1 = more creative.
  • Chain-of-Thought: Explicit step-by-step reasoning.
