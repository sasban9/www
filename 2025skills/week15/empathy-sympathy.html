💞 Empathy vs. Sympathy

📘 What You’ll Learn

  • 🤝 The difference between empathy and sympathy
  • 💬 Why empathy builds stronger connections
  • 🌱 How to practice empathy in daily life

🧐 Understanding the Concepts

Empathy is the ability to feel with someone — to understand and share their emotions as if they were your own. 💗

✨ Example: "I can imagine how hard that must be for you."

Sympathy is feeling sorry for someone’s situation but staying emotionally distant. 😕

💭 Example: "That sounds rough. Hope you feel better soon."

🎲 Quick Quiz: Is It Empathy or Sympathy?

Write "Empathy" or "Sympathy" for each of the following:

  1. “Wow, I’ve been through something similar. I totally get it.”
  2. “That sucks. Poor you.”
  3. “I can’t imagine what you’re going through, but I’m here to listen.”
  4. “That’s sad. Anyway, want to hang out?”

📓 Journal Prompt

✍️ Describe a time you offered someone empathy. What did you say or do?

🚀 Try It Today

Think of a friend or family member who might need emotional support. Practice empathy by doing one of the following:

  • 👂 Listening without interrupting
  • 💬 Asking how they truly feel
  • 🤗 Sharing a time you felt something similar
